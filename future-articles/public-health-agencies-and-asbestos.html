Public Health Agencies and Asbestos Guidance

Which U.S. agencies regulate, track, or publish asbestos and mesothelioma information, and what each one covers.

Updated March 11, 2026 5 min read Stored separately for later publication.

Different agencies handle different parts of the asbestos story. OSHA focuses on workplace exposure standards. EPA addresses environmental oversight, legacy uses, and building-management guidance. CDC publishes surveillance and cancer statistics, while the National Cancer Institute and other public-health bodies provide cancer education and risk information. OSHA EPA CDC
